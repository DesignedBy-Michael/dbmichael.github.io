About Me

I'm a Brooklyn-born designer with a passion for creating brands that tell stories and connect deeply with audiences. Growing up in the Y2K era, I was heavily influenced by anime and the vibrant culture of New York City, which shaped my creative eye and love for bold, nostalgic design.

In 2004, my family moved to Florida, and that mix of cultures—Brooklyn's grit and Florida's laid-back vibe—deeply impacted how I see the world and approach design.

My journey into design began during the 2020 lock downs, where I discovered my love for design thinking and honed my skills through hands-on experience, like volunteering for an e-sports team. That experience taught me the importance of deadlines, budgets, and the marketing side of design—skills I later refined by going back to school and graduating in 2024.

But my story doesn't start there. Before design, I served in the Marine Corps,stationed at Kaneohe Bay, Hawaii. Immersed in the rich cultures of Hawaii and the diverse backgrounds of my fellow Marines, I gained a deeper appreciation for perspective, connection, and the power of storytelling. Those lessons, along with the discipline and resilience I learned in the Corps, still guide me today—whether I'm leading as a design instructor assistant or working closely with clients to bring their visions to life.

For me, design is more than just creating visuals—it's about being of service. I know how important it is to have a strong brand in today's online world, and I see myself as a guide, helping my clients build the dream brands they deserve. From structured processes to creative problem-solving, I'm here to make the journey seamless and impactful.

When I'm not designing, you'll find me embracing the outdoors, running, or hiking—activities that keep me grounded and inspired. I believe in designing not just brands, but a life filled with purpose, clarity, and connection.

Let's build something unforgettable together.
